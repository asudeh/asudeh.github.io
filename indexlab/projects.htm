Mithra

Projects related to Responsible Data Science and Algorithmic Fairness

Terms such as machine bias, algorithmic fairness, and data ethics have become popular as big data technologies constantly fail to fulfill their promise of bringing good to society. Google gorilla incident and machine bias in tools used by judges are only two of the many examples. Biased data-driven decision making harms individuals and imapacts society every day, and we (the computer scientists) are to be (partially) blamed.

In order to prevent Big Data technologies' potential harms, and to ensure that matters such as fairness, stability, accountability, and transparency are fulfilled, it is critical to revisit the life cycle of data and to develop proper tools, strategies, and metrics. This is our target in Mithra.

Fact Checking

Projects related to Computational Fact Checking

This is an interesting topic we have recently started exploring. Particularly, we pay attention that fake news may not always be a complete fabrication. Misleading claims are often made by cherry-picking data. This is popular among politicians since they would like not to be caught blatantly lying. That is why "A lie which is half a truth is ever the blackest of lies" (A.Tennyson).
But how to computationally detect/measure "half a truth"? We aim to address this question in this project.

Web DataX

Projects related to Web Data Exploration

Web databases, also known as deep web or hidden web databases, cover a large portion of web. This includes shopping websites such as Amazon and eBay, service search sites such as Expedia and Google Flights, recommendation websites, and P2P marketplaces such as Airbnb and Craigslist. With minor differences, such systems have a typical structure that enforce a ranked retrieval interface.
Our projects in this category enable efficient data exploration and flexible query answering and recommendation in this environment.
